History

The color orange derived its name from the fruit of the same name. Before the English language adopted the word "orange," the color was often referred to as "geoluhread" in Old English, which means "yellow-red. Orange is considered one of the newest additions to the color spectrum in human history. Ancient civilizations didn't have a specific term for the color and often used a combination of red and yellow to represent what we now know as orange."

Symbolism in Cultures

In various cultures, orange holds symbolic significance. For instance, in Hinduism, orange is considered sacred and represents purity and spirituality. In some Asian cultures, orange symbolizes good luck, happiness, and prosperity. Orange is also a prominent color in various cultural celebrations. In the Netherlands, the Dutch wear orange clothing and accessories during "Koningsdag" (King's Day) to honor the royal family. Similarly, in Ireland, orange represents the Protestant community and is associated with the Orange Order parades.

In the realm of safety, orange plays a crucial role. High-visibility clothing, such as safety vests or construction site markers, often feature orange hues. This color helps increase visibility and draws attention, making it essential for maintaining safety and preventing accidents.
